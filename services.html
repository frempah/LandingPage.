05 — Questions

COMMON
QUESTIONS

How do I pay and is it safe? +
I accept payment via Wise (international bank transfer), PayPal, and Paystack. The standard structure is 50% upfront to begin work and 50% on final delivery. You never pay the full amount before seeing the finished product. Receipts are sent for every transaction.
Do you work with international clients? +
Absolutely — the majority of freelance work happens remotely. I work across time zones using async communication (Slack, Notion, email) and schedule video calls via Zoom or Google Meet. Location is never a barrier.
What if I don't like the first design? +
Every project includes revision rounds — the Professional tier includes 3 rounds, Enterprise includes unlimited. We always start with wireframes for approval before the high-fidelity design, so there are no big surprises. You review and approve at every stage before we move forward.
Do I own the code after delivery? +
Yes — full intellectual property (IP) transfers to you upon final payment. All source code is pushed to your own GitHub repository. You own everything: the design files, the codebase, the deployed product. I retain nothing.
How long does a typical project take? +
A landing page takes 1–2 weeks. A full website 2–4 weeks. A mobile app 4–8 weeks. Timeline depends on project scope and how quickly you provide feedback and content. A clear brief at the start = faster delivery every time.
Can you maintain my site after launch? +
Yes — all tiers include post-launch support (14–60 days depending on tier). After that, I offer monthly retainer packages for ongoing maintenance, content updates, and feature additions. Ask about retainer pricing during our discovery call.
What do I need to prepare before hiring you? +
The more prepared you are, the faster and better the result. Ideally bring: a brief describing what you want, any brand assets (logo, colours, fonts), content (text and images), and examples of sites you like. If you don't have these, I can help you define them during discovery.
Do you sign NDAs or contracts? +
Yes — a project contract is signed before work begins on all projects. This protects both parties by clearly documenting scope, timeline, payment terms, and IP ownership. NDAs are available for Enterprise clients on request.
